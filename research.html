Research Projects

Sustainable Menstrual Hygiene Materials

Focus: Biomaterials, Sustainability, Global Health

This project explored using pineapple and sisal fibers as biodegradable, localized alternatives to cotton for menstrual pads in resource-limited regions. These fibers were modified through decortication and enzymatic delignification using laccase enzymes to enhance their absorbent properties.

Key Findings: Treated sisal fibers achieved absorbency levels competitive with traditional cotton, reaching an average ratio of 112.5 grams fluid/grams square. This research addresses both environmental concerns and period poverty through decentralized production.

Supramolecular Nanofibers for Mucosal Vaccines

Focus: Bioengineering, Immunology

Conducted through the Duke Research in Engineering Program, this work involved synthesizing supramolecular nanofibers for sublingual gonorrhea vaccines. The project included peptide synthesis purification, nanofiber characterization, and mucoadhesion analysis to develop heat-stable vaccine platforms.
