Welcome

I’m Prerana Kulla, a freshman at UNC-Chapel Hill from Harrisburg, NC. Before coming to Carolina, I graduated from the North Carolina School of Science and Mathematics at Morganton. Currently, I am pursuing a double major in Psychology and Data Science with a minor in Business of Health.

In high school, I founded a chapter of the Youth Movement Against Alzheimer's (YMAA), where I partnered with senior homes to foster meaningful connections between students and residents. Serving on the YMAA Youth Leadership Council inspired me to bring this mission to UNC. Additionally, I founded the Menstrual Movement in Morganton to combat period poverty, especially in the wake of Hurricane Helene. This initiative included a menstrual drive that supported over 500 women and the publication of Red Ribbon, a children’s book designed to amplify advocacy narratives and educate adolescents.

On campus, I serve as a Senior Residential Computing Consultant (RCC) with ResNET. I am also an active member of Carolina Women in Business, CPALS, and Bharat Sangeet. I work as a Research Assistant at the Schiller Lab, where I collaborate on data collection via brain scans and blood draws to study neural and molecular responses to reward in women.

Looking ahead, I hope to gain more experience in psychiatric research specifically focused on women's health.
